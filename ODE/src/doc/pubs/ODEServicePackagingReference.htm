    Introduction

    The IBM Open Development Environment (ODE) provides a method for developers to prepare service packages for installation against existing product installations.

    Service Packaging with ODE

    Overview

    ODE supports two main types of packaging; initial product packaging (IPP) and service packaging.  IPP is a full and complete package, containing the entire contents of a product.  Service packaging is generally a partial, or incremental set of files used to update or refresh an existing software installation.  The most important difference between initial product packaging and service packaging is that not all parts are necessarily included in a service package.  This section of will discuss some of the features of service packaging.

    Fix Strategies

    The determination and collection of the shippable parts is required for service packaging. What parts should be collected is determined by interrogating the fix strategy. There are currently two fix strategies that are supported by ODE:

         refresh - the whole product is shipped for service
         cumulative - the parts changed for this build, plus any parts changed in previous builds, up to, but not including the original product shipment, are included in the service package (cumulative has two types - fix and maintenance)

    The fix strategy chosen has interesting implications for the complexity and size of the installable packages to be produced. For example, a refresh strategy implies the complete superseding of a previously shipped package, resulting in a package that is the same size as the original product shipment.  The cumulative strategy implies a forced supersede, but a smaller installable package than the original product. A product will most likely select a fix strategy and stick with it for the life of the product; however, there are some platforms which alternate between fix and maintenance type cumulative fix strategies. This allows the product owner to "clean up" a serviceable entity if its cumulative package becomes as large as the initial product package, and start the service stream over again.
